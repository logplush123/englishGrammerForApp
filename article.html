Mastering Articles (A, An, The)

Introduction to Articles

Articles are the three words: **'a'**, **'an'**, and **'the'**. They are a type of **determiner** used before a noun (or a noun phrase) to specify whether the noun is general or specific.

They are categorized into two types:

  • **Indefinite Articles:** 'a' and 'an' (अनिश्चितता सूचक)
  • **Definite Article:** 'the' (निश्चितता सूचक)

Indefinite Articles: 'A' and 'An'

These are used when referring to a **non-specific** or **general** singular countable noun. Both 'a' and 'an' mean 'one' or 'any' of a particular kind.

  • Use of 'A': Used before a singular countable noun that begins with a **consonant sound** (not letter).

    e.g., a book, a house, a European (starts with a 'y' sound), a union (starts with a 'y' sound).

  • Use of 'An': Used before a singular countable noun that begins with a **vowel sound** (not letter).

    e.g., an ant, an image, an ox, an honest man (starts with an 'o' sound), an M.A. (starts with an 'em' sound).

  • The deciding factor is the SOUND, not the spelling.
  • **A/An is NOT used before:**
    1. Uncountable nouns (e.g., water, honesty, milk).
    2. Plural nouns (e.g., boxes, boys).
    3. Proper nouns (e.g., Kathmandu, Ram).

Definite Article: 'The'

'The' indicates a **specific** noun. Unlike 'a/an', 'the' can be used before **singular, plural, countable, and uncountable** nouns, as long as they are specified or unique.

Common Uses of 'The':
  • Before a noun that has been **mentioned before** (specific context): Ram bought a radio. The radio is very good.
  • Before **unique objects** (only one exists): the sun, the moon, the earth.
  • Before **superlatives** and **ordinals**: the biggest, the highest, the first, the second.
  • Before **names of specific geographical features**: seas, rivers, mountain ranges (plural), island groups, plural country names (e.g., the Pacific Ocean, the Himalayas, the USA, the Philippines).
  • Before an adjective to refer to a **group of people**: the poor, the rich.
  • Before a noun made specific by a **phrase or clause**: The man in the black suit. The girl who helped me.
  • Before **specific institutions/places when used for a secondary purpose**: We went to the hospital to visit a friend. (Primary purpose is to receive treatment).
Common Omissions (No Article):
  • Before most **proper nouns**: countries, towns, person names (e.g., Nepal, Paris, Nita).
  • Before general **abstract nouns**: death, honesty, happiness (used in a general sense).
  • Before the names of **meals** (used generally): breakfast, lunch, dinner.
  • Before **mountain peaks** (singular), days, or months: Mt. Everest, Sunday, January.
  • Before **institutions when used for their primary purpose**: He goes to school to study. She is in hospital.
