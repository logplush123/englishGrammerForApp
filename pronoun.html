ENGLISH GRAMMAR: PRONOUNS

Pronoun (सर्वनाम)

A **Pronoun** is a word that is used **in place of a noun** (or noun phrase) to avoid its repetition. (सर्वनाम: नाम (Noun) को सट्टामा प्रयोग हुने शब्दलाई सर्वनाम भनिन्छ, जसले गर्दा नाम बारम्बार दोहोरिनु पर्दैन।)

Examples of Pronoun usage:

She is Sita. We are friends. You are Rajan. This is a pen.

Types of Pronouns (सर्वनामका प्रकारहरू)

1. Personal Pronoun (व्यक्तिगत सर्वनाम)

These pronouns are used for specific persons, animals, objects, or groups. They change their form based on the person (1st, 2nd, 3rd) and case (Subjective, Objective, Possessive). (यी सर्वनामहरू व्यक्ति, जनावर वा वस्तुको लागि प्रयोग गरिन्छन् । तिनीहरू पुरुष (Person) र कारक (Case) अनुसार फरक हुन्छन् ।)

Case of Personal Pronouns (व्यक्तिगत सर्वनामको कारक)

Person Subjective (कर्ता) Objective (कर्म) Possessive Adjective (P1) + Noun Possessive Pronoun (P2)
1st (Singular) I Me My (e.g., *My book*) Mine
1st (Plural) We Us Our (e.g., *Our house*) Ours
2nd (S/P) You You Your (e.g., *Your pen*) Yours
3rd (Singular) He, She, It Him, Her, It His, Her, Its His, Hers
3rd (Plural) They Them Their Theirs

**Note:** Possessive Adjectives (P1: *my, our, your*, etc.) must always be followed by a noun. Possessive Pronouns (P2: *mine, ours, yours*, etc.) can stand alone. (ध्यान दिनुहोस्: Possessive Adjectives (P1) पछि सधैं संज्ञा (Noun) आउँछ, तर Possessive Pronouns (P2) एक्लै प्रयोग हुन सक्छ।)

Example: He helped me giving his book.

2. Reflexive and Emphatic Pronouns (निजवाचक र जोडदार सर्वनाम)

These are the **self/selves** forms of pronouns (*myself, ourselves, yourself, yourselves, himself, herself, itself, themselves*). The only difference is in their **function** in a sentence. (यी सर्वनामहरू 'self' वा 'selves' बाट बनेका हुन्छन् । यिनीहरूको कामको आधारमा फरक छुट्याइन्छ ।)

Reflexive Pronoun (निजवाचक)

The action returns or **reflects** upon the subject. It always functions as the **object** of the verb or a preposition and is **essential** to the sentence meaning. (यसले कर्तामा फर्केर आउने कामलाई जनाउँछ र वाक्यको अर्थको लागि आवश्यक हुन्छ।)

Example: She cooked **herself** a meal.

Emphatic Pronoun (जोडदार)

Used only for **emphasis** (जोड दिन). It typically comes immediately after the noun/pronoun it emphasizes and can be **removed** without destroying the core meaning of the sentence. (यसले संज्ञा वा सर्वनामलाई **जोड दिन** मात्र प्रयोग हुन्छ र यसलाई हटाउँदा पनि वाक्यको मुख्य अर्थ बिग्रँदैन।)

Example: He **himself** did it.

3. Demonstrative Pronouns (सङ्केतक सर्वनाम)

These pronouns (**This, That, These, Those, Such**) are used to **point out** specific objects or persons. (यी सर्वनामहरू कुनै खास वस्तु वा व्यक्तिलाई **सङ्केत गर्न** प्रयोग गरिन्छन् ।)

  • **This** (singular) and **These** (plural) point out **nearest** things. (यो र यी नजिकका लागि)
  • **That** (singular) and **Those** (plural) point out **farther** things. (त्यो र ती टाढाका लागि)

4. Distributive Pronouns (वितरण सूचक सर्वनाम)

Distributive pronouns (**Each, Either, Neither**) refer to persons or things **one at a time**, even if they are part of a group. They are always followed by a **singular verb**. (यसले समूहमा रहेका व्यक्ति वा वस्तुहरूलाई **एक-एक गरेर** बुझाउँछ र सधैं singular क्रिया (verb) लिन्छ ।)

  • **Each** of the girls **was** beautiful.
  • **Either** of you **can** go.
  • **Neither** of the pens **is** hers.

5. Interrogative Pronouns (प्रश्नवाचक सर्वनाम)

These pronouns (**Who, Whom, Whose, Which, What**) are used to **ask questions**. When they stand alone and ask a question, they function as pronouns. (यी सर्वनामहरू **प्रश्न सोध्न** प्रयोग हुन्छन् ।)

  • **What** is your name?
  • **Who** is he to you?
  • **Which** is the best book?

6. Relative Pronouns (सम्बन्धवाचक सर्वनाम)

These pronouns (**Who, Whom, Whose, Which, That**) are used to **connect** a clause (a relative/adjective clause) to a noun or pronoun (the antecedent) earlier in the sentence. (यी सर्वनामहरू वाक्यमा पहिले आएको संज्ञा (Noun) वा सर्वनाम (Pronoun) सँग पछिको वाक्यको भागलाई **जोड्न** प्रयोग हुन्छन् ।)

  • Neha, **whom** I helped a lot, also helped me.
  • Pooja, **whose** pencil was lost yesterday, is very cooperative.
  • Nepal, **which** is a republic state, is a landlocked country.

7. Reciprocal Pronouns (आपसी सर्वनाम)

These pronouns (**each other / one another**) express a **mutual action or relationship** between two or more subjects. (यी सर्वनामहरूले दुई वा सो भन्दा बढी कर्ताहरू बीचको **आपसी वा पारस्परिक सम्बन्ध** देखाउँछन् ।)

  • **Each other** is typically used for **two** people/things. (e.g., Poonam and Mona help **each other**.)
  • **One another** is typically used for **more than two** people/things. (e.g., The students help **one another**.)

8. Indefinite Pronouns (अनिश्चित सर्वनाम)

These pronouns (**someone, anybody, everything, nobody, all, few, many, several**) refer to non-specific people, objects, or amounts. (यी सर्वनामहरूले कुनै निश्चित व्यक्ति वा वस्तुलाई नजनाई अनिश्चित रूपमा जनाउँछन् ।)

  • **Somebody** is knocking at the door.
  • I ate **everything** on my plate.
