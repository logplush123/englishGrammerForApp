Adverbs

Adverbs are words (or phrases/clauses) that **modify** or provide additional information about a **verb**, an **adjective**, or another **adverb**. They typically answer questions like *How*, *When*, *Where*, *To what extent*, or *How often*.

Key Examples:

  • Ram runs fast (Modifies the verb *runs*).
  • He is very good (Modifies the adjective *good*).
  • She drives incredibly slowly (Modifies the adverb *slowly*).
  • I will come afterwards (Adverb of Time).
  • You can't go away (Adverb of Place).
  • He hardly comes here (Adverb of Frequency).

Adjective + -ly = Adverb

Many adverbs are formed by adding -ly to an adjective.

Nice (Adj) $\rightarrow$ Nicely (Adv)
Quick (Adj) $\rightarrow$ Quickly (Adv)
Sincere (Adj) $\rightarrow$ Sincerely (Adv)
Polite (Adj) $\rightarrow$ Politely (Adv)

Degrees of Comparison

Adjectives and some Adverbs can change form to show intensity. There are three main degrees:

  • Positive Degree: The base form, used for singular items or non-comparisons. (नेपाली: आधारभूत रूप, जुन एकल वस्तु वा तुलना नगरिएको अवस्थामा प्रयोग गरिन्छ।) Example: The water is cold.
  • Comparative Degree: Used to compare **two** things. Often formed by adding -er or using more/less. (नेपाली: **दुई** वस्तुहरू तुलना गर्न प्रयोग गरिन्छ। प्राय: $ -er $ जोडेर वा $ more/less $ प्रयोग गरेर बनाइन्छ।) Example: The pool water is colder than the ocean.
  • Superlative Degree: Used to compare **three or more** things. Often formed by adding -est or using most/least. (नेपाली: **तीन वा सोभन्दा बढी** वस्तुहरू तुलना गर्न प्रयोग गरिन्छ। प्राय: $ -est $ जोडेर वा $ most/least $ प्रयोग गरेर बनाइन्छ।) Example: The ice water is the coldest drink of all.
