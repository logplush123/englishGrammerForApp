Mastering the Noun

A **noun** is a word used to identify any of a class of people, places, or things, or to name a particular one of these. Nouns are generally categorized into five primary groups, as explored below:

1. Proper Noun üåü

A **Proper Noun** is the specific name of a particular person, place, or thing.

Key Rule: The first letter of a Proper Noun is always capitalized.

  • **Sita** is eating. (Specific person)
  • **Kalaiya** is a town. (Specific place)
  • The **Bagmati** is a river. (Specific river)
  • The **Ramayan** is an epic. (Specific book)

2. Common Noun üë•

A **Common Noun** is a name given in common to every person, place, or thing of the same class or kind; it represents the whole species.

  • It is a **dog**.
  • The **elephant** is an **animal**.
  • It is a **pencil**.
  • I have a **computer**.

Note: A Common Noun often requires an article (a, an, the) before it when singular.

3. Collective Noun üßë‚Äçü§ù‚Äçüßë

A **Collective Noun** names a group or collection of people, animals, or things as a single unit.

  • I saw a **gang** of robbers.
  • I lost a **bunch** of keys.
  • A **crowd** of people was there.
  • A **herd** of deer is grazing.

More Collective Nouns:

  • a herd of cattle
  • a pride of lions
  • a swarm of butterflies
  • a bouquet of flowers
  • a school/shoal of fish
  • an army of soldiers
  • a panel of experts
  • a colony of ants
  • a galaxy of stars

4. Material Noun üß±

A **Material Noun** refers to the matter or substance (solid, liquid, or gas) from which things are made or that exists naturally.

  • She eats **meat**.
  • I boil **water**.
  • They cooked **curry**.
  • I bought some **rice** and **salt**.
  • The ring is made of **gold**.

Note: We typically **cannot** use 'a' or 'an' before a Material Noun. 'The' may be used when the material is specialized (e.g., "The water in this well is pure.").

5. Abstract Noun üí°

An **Abstract Noun** names a quality, state, idea, or action that cannot be perceived by the five senses (i.e., you cannot see, hear, taste, touch, or smell it).

These nouns represent concepts or feelings.

  • He felt great **relief**.
  • I was surprised at his **stupidity**.
  • His **poverty** made him beg.
  • Her **beauty** is praiseworthy.
  • His **nervousness** is his enemy.

Summary: Abstract Nouns cannot be seen or touched; the other four noun types generally can be observed.
