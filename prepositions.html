Prepositions

Prepositions (नामयोगी) are words often used before a noun to show its relation to the other part of a clause. There are two kinds of prepositions: prepositions of place and prepositions of time But certain prepositions are used both as prepositions of place and time. See the following sentences:

  • I live in Baglung. (in: preposition of place)
  • I was born in 1990 AD. ( in: preposition of time)

Usage: Common Prepositions

'At' , 'on' and 'in' are the most common prepositions which are used as both prepositions of time and prepositions of place.

  • 'at' is used to denote a fixed place or fixed time (clock time):at the airport, at the gate, at 9:30, at midnight, (also at night, at Christmas, at Dashain)

  • 'in' is used to indicate an inside position or a period of time: in the morning, in 1990, in July, in summer, in Pokhara, in a room, in the ground

  • 'on' is used to indicate a surface position or a particular day: on the table, on the wall, on the roof, on the head, on Saturday, on 12th August, 2010, on my birthday

  • 'into' and 'onto' are used for direction or position in motion: pour milk into the bottle, go onto the park

  • 'over' denotes the position vertically up while 'under' denotes the position vertically down: the bridge over the river, plane over the mountains, cat under the chair.

  • 'below' indicates the downward position which is not joined by a vertical line, 'above' is used for upward position in the same way: field below the village, the blackboard above the benches in a classroom

Use of more prepositions:

  1. The tree is in front of the car. (ahead of)

  2. The car is behind the house. (at back of)

  3. He fell off a ladder. (fell down)

  4. Jump off the wall. (crossed over)

  5. Turn off the radio. (switched off, stopped operating)

  6. The plane took off. (left the ground and flew)

  7. They went down the hill. (going to downward position)

  8. Cut the fruit with a knife. (instrument)

  9. Rita went to Dolpa with her father. (accompanied by)

  10. I met a man with a crooked nose. (having)

  11. Did you see a man with an umbrella?(carrying)

  12. They went to Kathmandu. (direction of movement)

  13. I am from Baglung. (origin)

  14. They studied the book from page 50. (starting point)

  15. She is suffering from fever. (cause of illness)

  16. Ram is between his parents. (position with one parent on each side.)

  17. The house is among the trees. (amidst several trees)

  18. We came back before midnight. (earlier than)

  19. Go out after meal. (later than)

  20. He went to school through the jungle. (movement usually from one side to the other and disappearing there)

  21. The children ran towards the jungle. (somewhere near)

  22. The thief ran across the bridge. (from one side to another)

  23. We walked along the river. (following course as a line)

  24. I have been working since early morning. (starting point)

  25. It has been raining for three days. (period of time consumed)

  26. I am going to Kathmandu for a week. (period of time to be spent)
