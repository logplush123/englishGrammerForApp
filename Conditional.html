CONDITIONAL SENTENCES

Structure and use of the four main conditional types.

Introduction

Conditional sentences express an action or event dependent on a specific condition. They typically consist of an **'if' clause** (the condition) and a **main clause** (the result). There are four main types of conditional sentences.

Type 0: General Truths

Used for facts that are always true, habits, or scientific laws.

IF/WHEN + Present Simple, Present Simple
सामान्य सत्य वा वैज्ञानिक तथ्य व्यक्त गर्न प्रयोग गरिन्छ।
  • If you speak the truth, everyone appreciates you.
  • If we heat water to 100 degrees Celsius, it boils.
  • If I am late to reach the office, my boss punishes me.

Type 1: Real Possibility

Used for a possible condition and its likely result in the future.

IF + Present Simple, WILL/MAY/MIGHT/COULD + Base Verb
भविष्यमा पूरा हुन सक्ने सम्भावना भएको अवस्था र त्यसको सम्भावित नतिजा देखाउँछ।
  • If you study hard, you will pass the exam.
  • If he is with you, you could win the world.
  • If I get a promotion next year, I may buy a new car.

Type 2: Unreal Present/Future

Used for hypothetical, counter-factual, or very low-probability conditions in the present or future.

IF + Past Simple, WOULD/COULD/MIGHT + Base Verb
वर्तमान वा भविष्यको काल्पनिक (कम सम्भावना भएको) अवस्था र त्यसको परिणाम व्यक्त गर्दछ।
  • If I were the manager, I would not go to this meeting.
  • If I won the lottery, I could buy a luxury car.
  • If you learned to speak English, you would definitely get this job.

Type 3: Unreal Past (Regret)

Used for a hypothetical past condition that did not happen, often expressing regret or criticism.

IF + Past Perfect, WOULD HAVE/COULD HAVE/MIGHT HAVE + Past Participle
विगतमा पूरा नभएको अवस्था र त्यसको अपूरणीय नतिजा (पछुतप) व्यक्त गर्दछ।
  • If he had studied well, he would have become a doctor.
  • If she had informed me earlier, I could have helped her.
  • If I hadn't seen his picture, I could have never identified him.
