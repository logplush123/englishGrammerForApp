ENGLISH GRAMMAR: CLAUSES

The building blocks of complex sentences.

What is a Clause?

A **clause** is a fundamental unit of grammar. It is a group of words that **contains a subject and a verb**. A clause may form a simple sentence, or it may combine with other clauses to form complex sentences.

परिभाषा: Clause भनेको शब्दहरूको यस्तो समूह हो जसमा एक **कर्ता (Subject)** र एक **क्रिया (Verb)** अनिवार्य रूपमा हुन्छ। यो अंग्रेजी वाक्यको आधारभूत एकाई हो।

Initial Examples:

  1. He is Sanjay.
  2. He was calling me today.
  3. He is Sanjay, who is Nitin's brother and an intelligent person. (Contains two clauses)
  4. Reading is a good habit.

The Two Main Types

  1. Independent Clauses
  2. Dependent Clauses

1. Independent Clause (Main Clause)

This group of words contains a subject and a verb and **makes complete sense on its own** as a sentence.

स्वतन्त्र उपवाक्य: यो शब्दहरूको समूह हो जसको आफ्नै पूर्ण अर्थ हुन्छ र यो आफैमा एक स्वतन्त्र वाक्यको रूपमा खडा हुन सक्छ।

Examples:

  • He is Sanjay.
  • Sita is a tall girl.
  • Sam went to the park.

2. Dependent Clause (Subordinate Clause)

This group of words contains a subject and a verb, but it **does NOT make complete sense on its own**. It must be attached to an Independent Clause.

आश्रित उपवाक्य: यो शब्दहरूको समूह हो जसमा Subject र Verb हुन्छ, तर यसको पूर्ण अर्थ बुझ्नको लागि यसलाई Independent Clause मा निर्भर रहनुपर्छ।

Types of Dependent Clauses:

  1. Noun Clause: Functions as a noun. (e.g., *what*, *that*, *who*)
  2. Adjective Clause (Relative Clause): Functions as an adjective, modifying a noun. (e.g., *who*, *which*, *that*, *where*)
  3. Adverb Clause: Functions as an adverb, modifying a verb, adjective, or adverb. (e.g., *because*, *when*, *if*, *although*)

Noun Clause

A clause that takes the place of a noun. It can be the subject or object of the main verb.

  • Can you guess what I have in my pocket?
  • Everyone can easily understand that you don't know the answer.
  • I don't know who you are and how you perform your duties.

Adjective Clause (Relative Clause)

A clause that modifies (describes) a noun or pronoun in the main clause.

  • Those students, who focused on their studies sincerely, passed the exam. (Describes 'students')
  • He is my brother, whom you had rejected in the interview last year. (Describes 'brother')
  • He loves Bihar, where he was born and spent his entire childhood. (Describes 'Bihar')

Adverb Clause

A clause that modifies the verb in the main clause. It answers the questions: **How, Why, When, Where,** or **To what extent?**

Adverb Clause: यसले मुख्य वाक्यको क्रिया (Verb) लाई परिमार्जन गर्दछ र प्रायः **किन, कहिले, कहाँ, कसरी** भन्ने जस्ता प्रश्नहरूको उत्तर दिन्छ।
  • I will meet you after the meeting finishes. (Answers: *When?*)
  • Because it was raining, we stayed indoors. (Answers: *Why?*)
  • She left the party as soon as she saw him. (Answers: *When?*)
