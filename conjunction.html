ENGLISH GRAMMAR: CONJUNCTIONS

Joining words, phrases, and clauses elegantly.

What is a Conjunction?

A **conjunction** is a connecting word that joins words, phrases, or clauses (sentences). They help create complex and elegant sentences, ensuring the flow of ideas is smooth and logical.

परिभाषा: दुई वा दुई भन्दा बढी शब्दहरू, वाक्यांशहरू (Phrases), वा उपवाक्यहरू (Clauses) लाई जोड्ने काम गर्ने शब्दलाई संयोजक (Conjunction) भनिन्छ । यसले वाक्यमा विचारहरूको सम्बन्ध स्पष्ट गर्न मद्दत गर्दछ ।

Examples in action:

  1. Hari and Ram are brothers.
  2. Two plus two equals four.
  3. Ajay is poor but honest.
  4. I drink water because I was thirsty.

Coordinating Conjunctions: FANBOYS

Coordinating conjunctions connect two equal elements (words, phrases, or independent clauses). The acronym **FANBOYS** is a classic way to remember them:

F - For A - And N - Nor B - But O - Or Y - Yet S - So

FANBOYS Examples:

  • He was tired and hungry.
  • I can't whistle, nor can I sing.
  • Jay studies hard, but he couldn't clear the exam.
  • I run fast, yet I came last.
  • I was hungry, so I ate the food.

Subordinating & Correlative Examples

Conjunctions also include correlative pairs (like 'neither...nor') and subordinating conjunctions (like 'because' or 'if').

  • She likes both pizza and pasta. (Correlative)
  • I woke up early so I could catch the sunrise. (Subordinating)
  • The weather was cold, yet we decided to go for a picnic.
  • She is neither tall nor short. (Correlative)
  • The book was interesting, but I couldn't finish it.
  • He wanted to play outside, but it started raining.
  • She spoke softly, yet her words had a powerful impact.
